# Employee Engagement Study — Apex Writing Instruments Pvt. Ltd.

---

## About the Project

For this project, I led a team of 11 members to consuct a data-driven employee engagement study conducted for Apex Writing Instruments Pvt. Ltd., a mid-sized Indian pen and pencil manufacturer that grew from ₹4,000 crores to ₹50,000 crores and is targeting ₹1,00,000 crores in the next two years. Despite strong business growth, the company was experiencing a significant and sustained decline in employee engagement across all departments and locations.

The study was commissioned by Mr. Rajesh, Vice President of Human Resources, and prepared for the Board of Directors. It covers the Production, Design, and Middle Management departments across four office locations — Delhi, Mumbai, Pune, and Hyderabad.

---

## Problem Statement

- Excessive workload and extended working hours due to aggressive scaling
- Lack of recognition and appreciation from leadership
- Poor career development opportunities and role clarity
- Communication gaps between employees and management
- Structural imbalance — 75% management-heavy workforce with too few execution-level staff
- Gender disparity — only 4.5% female employees, all in Senior Manager roles
- These issues led to increased errors, lower productivity, and declining product quality

---

## Methodology

- **Framework:** Utrecht Work Engagement Scale (UWES) — 8 dimensions across 3 constructs (Vigor, Dedication, Absorption)
- **Scale:** 7-point Likert Scale (1 = Strongly Disagree / Never → 7 = Strongly Agree / Always)
- **Data:** 311 employees surveyed annually from 2019 to 2023 — 1,557 total responses
- **Locations:** Delhi (152), Mumbai (103), Pune (36), Hyderabad (20)
- **Analysis Techniques:** Descriptive, comparative, trend, percentage, and dimensional analysis
- **Tools Used:** Microsoft Excel (data preparation), Power BI (dashboarding and visualisation)

---

## Key Findings

- Engagement never crossed the neutral midpoint (4.0) across all five years which points to a structural problem
- Overall engagement dropped from 3.06 in 2019 to 2.01 in 2023, a 34.31% overall decline
- 2021 was the crisis year in which all 8 dimensions fell below 2.0, with the overall average hitting 1.89 (coinciding with COVID-19 and aggressive growth targets simultaneously)
- A W-shaped (double-valley) trend shows that the partial recovery in 2022 was followed by another collapse in 2023, indicating no structural fix was implemented
- Senior Managers — the most experienced and highest-paid group recorded the lowest 5-year average engagement (2.31), showing seniority does not imply higher engagement
- Managers dropped to 1.69 in 2021 which is the lowest designation score in the entire study
- Mumbai has zero Team Leaders, leaving 30 Associates without supervisory support
- Higher pay does not equal higher engagement. ₹61K+ earners are the least engaged; ₹21–40K Team Leaders are the most engaged
- D5 (Pride in Work): 78% of employees chose "Strongly Disagree" (rating 1) in 2023, down from 34% being positively engaged in 2019
- Delhi saw the sharpest city-wise decline at 38.7% over five years

---

## Recommendations (4-Phase Roadmap)

**Phase 1 — Immediate (0–3 months):** Department-wise team huddles, anonymous pulse surveys, urgent workload audit, role clarity charters for all managers

**Phase 2 — Short-Term (3–9 months):** Multi-tiered recognition framework, monthly town halls and team huddles, targeted structural intervention in Delhi and Mumbai, engagement-linked KPIs for managers (20% of performance rating)

**Phase 3 — Medium-Term (9–18 months):** Three-tier training and leadership development programme, individual development plans, Employee Happiness Programme (counselling, wellness, physical environment audit), gender diversity initiatives

**Phase 4 — Long-Term (18+ months):** Leaner organisational structure with broader execution base, formal succession planning framework, embedding continuous learning into performance evaluations
